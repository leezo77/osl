OSL - Operating Model

Purpose & Taxonomy

A clear, concise framework for the OutSystems Subject Leads (OSL) to bridge Product and Field teams.

Core Mission: The Three Pillars

The OSL team operates on three core functions designed to create a seamless feedback and enablement loop across the organization.

1

Dissemination

Translate product updates into clear, actionable assets for the field. Make new capabilities impossible to miss.

2

Intelligence

Capture structured feedback, competitive insights, and win/loss analysis from the field to inform the product roadmap.

3

Enablement

Deliver scalable training programs (101, 201, 301) to ensure field teams are confident and competent in capabilities.

Shared Taxonomy

Standardized labels are critical. Use these tags consistently across all tools (Salesforce, Productboard, Confluence).

Capability

Product-level feature. Ex: RAG, Guardrails, Tool Use, Orchestration.

Use Case

Customer problem solved. Ex: Claims Triage, Loan Pre-qualification.

Solution

Sellable bundle. Ex: "Agentic Claims Triage for Insurance" package.

Region

Geographic area. Ex: AMER, EMEA, APAC, ANZ, JP.

Maturity

Enablement level. Ex: 101 (Intro), 201 (Hands-on), 301 (Advanced).

Tool Setup

Specific configurations for our existing tool stack.

Role: Single Source of Truth

  • Space: OSL – Agentic
  • Structure: Home page with "What's New" feed. Parent pages for Capabilities, Solutions, Competitive, and Enablement.
  • Templates: Use standardized templates for Capability Briefs, Solution Playbooks, and Battlecards.
  • Labels: Enforce taxonomy labels (e.g., `capability-rag`, `region-apac`) for searchability.
  • Governance: Each page must have an owner and a "last reviewed" date. Archive old content aggressively.

Role: Feedback Intake & Triage

  • Feedback Portal: "OSL – Field Feedback (Agentic)"
  • Required Fields: Region, SFDC Opportunity Link, Capability/Use Case, Fit (Good/Partial/Poor), Gap Description, Competitors.
  • Triage SLA: 5 business days for regional OSL to enrich, tag, and link to a feature.
  • Synthesis: OSL aggregates patterns into a "Top 5 Gaps" collection, reviewed monthly with Product.

Role: Real-time Communication

  • #agentic-announcements: Read-only. For official OSL updates.
  • #agentic-field-intel: Open channel with a Workflow Form that pushes feedback directly to the Productboard portal.
  • Regional Channels: e.g., `#agentic-anz`. For local discussions, wins, and clinic invites.
  • Pins: Pin links to Confluence, Productboard portal, and demo environments in relevant channels.

Role: Business Context & Reporting

  • Custom Fields (Opportunity): Create picklists for `Agentic_Use_Case__c`, `Agentic_Capability__c`, `Agentic_Fit__c`, and `Agentic_Competitors__c`.
  • Reporting: Build dashboards to track pipeline influenced by Agentic, win rates vs. competitors, and common product gaps by region.
  • Automation: When `Agentic_Fit__c` is "Poor", auto-post a reminder to the relevant Slack channel to submit detailed feedback.

Core Artifacts

The key documents produced by the OSL team. Standardize with Confluence templates.

Capability Brief (1-Pager)

Translates a product feature for the field.

  • What it is & when to use it
  • Prerequisites & limitations
  • 3-step demo script
  • Top 5 FAQs

Solution Playbook

A guide to selling a bundled solution.

  • Target problem & customer fit
  • Reference architecture
  • Demo kit & implementation steps
  • Bill of Materials (BOM) & security notes

Competitive Battlecard

How to win against specific competitors.

  • Where they win / Where we win
  • Landmine discovery questions
  • Objection handling & counters
  • Approved claims with proof points

Win/Loss Brief

Quick analysis post-deal to capture insights.

  • SFDC link & deal summary
  • Primary reason for win/loss
  • What helped (features/assets)
  • What hurt (gaps/competitors)

Communication Cadence

A predictable rhythm of meetings and async updates.

Weekly

OSL Global Sync (45 min)

Topics: Field wins/signals, roadmap updates, top gaps, action items.

Bi-Weekly

Regional Enablement Clinic (45-60 min)

Topics: 101/201 training rotation, live demo practice, deal Q&A.

Monthly

Product Partnership (60 min)

Topics: Present "Top 5 Gaps" with SFDC $ evidence, roadmap alignment.

Quarterly

Strategic Review (90 min)

Topics: Regional performance, win/loss themes, competitive landscape, solution roadmap.

Enablement Ladder

A structured path to build field competency.

101

Introduction

The "what" and "why". Includes a 5-min demo and a 1-pager. Certification via quiz.

201

Hands-On

Build and configure. Includes a hands-on lab. Certification via demo checklist.

301

Advanced

Architecture & production. Includes hardening for security/scale. Certification via practical exam.

KPIs & OKRs

Measuring what matters to track success and impact.

Adoption

  • % of field completing 201 certification
  • # of demos run per region/month

Business Impact

  • Pipeline influenced by Agentic ($)
  • Win rate vs. top 3 competitors
  • % of opportunities with "Good" fit rating

Feedback Loop Health

  • Avg. time from feedback submission to triage
  • % of feedback notes with SFDC link
  • # of roadmap items influenced

Example OKR (Q4)

Objective: Achieve field readiness for Agentic in all regions.

  • KR1: Certify 80% of pre-sales globally on Agentic-201.
  • KR2: Source 30 new qualified opportunities tagged with an Agentic use case.
  • KR3: Reduce feedback triage time from 10 days to 5 days.

Action Plan

A phased rollout plan to implement this operating model.

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 0-30)

  • Configure Confluence space, pages, and templates.
  • Set up Slack channels and workflow form.
  • Configure Productboard portal, tags, and triage SLA.
  • Pilot 2 Capability Briefs and 1 Solution Playbook.
  • Run first "101 Clinic" in one pilot region (e.g., APAC).

Phase 2: Activation (Days 31-60)

  • Add 3-5 more Capability Briefs.
  • Publish v1 of Competitive Battlecards.
  • Activate Salesforce custom fields and dashboards.
  • Roll out bi-weekly regional clinics globally.
  • Hold first monthly Product partnership review.

Phase 3: Scale & Optimize (Days 61-90)

  • Launch "201 Hands-On" certification path.
  • Publish first quarterly competitive brief.
  • Conduct first quarterly strategic review.
  • Review OKRs and adjust for the next quarter.
  • Gather feedback on the operating model itself.
